So, the [login details of around 10,000 Hotmail accounts were posted to the web](http://www.theinquirer.net/inquirer/news/1557535/hotmail-suffers-massive-theft) and [subsequent analysis reveals that the most commonly used password is &#8220;123456&#8221;](http://www.theinquirer.net/inquirer/news/1557915/top-passwords-revealed). Clearly that's a terribly silly password to use but let's look at the details:Â There were 64 instances of &#8220;123456&#8221; in 10,000 records. 0.006% is not a significant number. Also, we have no idea how many of these Hotmail accounts were &#8220;genuine&#8221;, by which i mean, those used regularly by the same person. After all, there must be quite a percentage of accounts (larger than 0.006%?) which are simply setup and used once to get through some online registration wall or other, no?

I think what i'm trying to say is that, as stupid as we tech types like to imagine they are, most people actually aren't that thick.

However, if it is a revelation to you that &#8220;123456&#8221; is a bad password to employ, i suggest you read these password tips from [Google](http://gmailblog.blogspot.com/2009/10/choosing-smart-password.html) and [F-Secure](http://www.f-secure.com/weblog/archives/00001691.html).

Also, don't use anything you find [on defaultpassword.com](http://defaultpassword.com) or from this &#8220;List of passwords that should NEVER be used&#8221; (originally posted on PClinuxonline.com but no longer available, as far as i can see)&#8230;
